# Why this structure is better

This keeps every KB focused.

Instead of:

```text
One giant messy knowledge phase
```

we get:

```text
shared platform
shared schema
shared approval/retrieval system
one domain KB per phase
consistent certification
consistent handoff contracts
```

So the final roadmap becomes:

```text
Phase 1 — Knowledge Foundation
Phase 2 — Source & Evidence System
Phase 3 — Knowledge Extraction System
Phase 4 — Knowledge Review & Approval System

Phase 5 — Cinematography Intelligence KB
Phase 6 — Lighting Intelligence KB
Phase 7 — Acting & Performance Intelligence KB
Phase 8 — Animation Acting & Motion Intelligence KB
Phase 9 — Editing & Assembly Intelligence KB
Phase 10 — Music Intelligence KB
Phase 11 — Sound Design & Foley Intelligence KB
Phase 12 — Voice Acting & Dialogue Performance KB
Phase 13 — Storytelling & Narrative Intelligence KB
Phase 14 — Audience Psychology & Age Suitability KB
Phase 15 — Visual Design, Art Direction & Worldbuilding KB
Phase 16 — Production Feasibility & Pipeline Intelligence KB
```

That is clean.

And the rule is:

```text
Every new KB phase reuses Phases 1–4.
Every KB phase follows the 5A–5T pattern.
Every domain is built one at a time.
Every domain produces certified, retrievable, agent-usable intelligence.
```

So yes — your suggestion is exactly right. Phase 5 is cinematography only, then the other KBs become their own phases.
